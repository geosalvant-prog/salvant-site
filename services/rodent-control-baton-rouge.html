Professional Rodent Control Services

Rodents such as rats and mice are common pests throughout Louisiana. Rodents can damage property, contaminate food storage areas, and create sanitation concerns inside homes and commercial facilities.

Salvant Environmental Services provides rodent control programs designed to identify rodent activity, remove existing infestations, and help prevent rodents from returning to the property.

Common Signs Of Rodent Activity

  • Scratching noises in walls or ceilings
  • Rodent droppings around food storage areas
  • Gnaw marks on wood, wiring, or containers
  • Rodent nesting materials in hidden areas
  • Unusual pet behavior near walls or cabinets

Rat Bait Boxes And Rodent Monitoring Stations

Rodent bait stations, often called rat boxes, are commonly used as part of professional rodent control programs. These secured bait stations are placed strategically around a property to help monitor and control rodent activity.

Bait stations are designed to target rodents while helping prevent access by pets or non-target animals. Regular inspection of bait stations allows technicians to monitor rodent activity and maintain effective control.

Rodent Trapping Programs

In some situations trapping may be used to remove rodents inside structures or areas where rodent activity is detected. Technicians determine the most appropriate control methods based on inspection findings and property conditions.

Rodent Prevention And Exclusion

Rodent control often includes identifying entry points around the property and conditions that attract rodents. Addressing these factors can help reduce the chances of future infestations.

Regular monitoring and preventative pest control services help maintain long-term rodent control.

Frequently Asked Questions

What rodents are common in Baton Rouge?

The most common rodents in Louisiana include roof rats, Norway rats, and house mice.

What are rodent bait stations?

Rodent bait stations are secured boxes used in professional pest control programs to monitor and control rodent activity around properties.

How do rodents get into buildings?

Rodents can enter buildings through small openings around foundations, doors, vents, and roof areas. Inspections help identify potential entry points.
